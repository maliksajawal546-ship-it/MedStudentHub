Clinicals of Upper Limbs

Comprehensive clinical examination guide for the upper limbs - essential for medical students.

Overview

The clinical examination of the upper limbs is a fundamental skill every medical student must master. This comprehensive guide covers anatomy, examination techniques, common pathologies, and clinical findings.

Key Topics

Anatomy of the Upper Limb

Bones, joints, muscles, nerves, and blood vessels of the shoulder, arm, forearm, and hand.

Clinical Examination Technique

Systematic approach to inspecting, palpating, and testing the upper limbs including strength and sensation assessments.

Common Pathologies

Fractures, dislocations, arthritis, nerve injuries, and vascular conditions affecting the upper limbs.

Physical Signs and Investigations

Important clinical signs, special tests, and imaging modalities used in upper limb examination.

Examination Sequence

  1. Inspection: Look for deformities, swelling, scars, and muscle wasting
  2. Palpation: Feel for tenderness, temperature, and crepitus
  3. Range of Motion: Assess active and passive movements
  4. Muscle Strength: Test power using grading scales (0-5)
  5. Neurovascular Assessment: Check sensation, reflexes, and pulse
  6. Special Tests: Perform specific maneuvers based on suspected pathology
